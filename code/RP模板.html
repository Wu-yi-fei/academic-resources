	My earlier work enabled me to have an in-depth understanding of not only how deep generative models (DGMs) can facilitate novel insights into renewable power scenarios,
	but also how these insights can be used to develop new discussions and solutions to decision-based problems using machine learning techniques.
	Therefore, to deal with the other two problems, my proposed research proposal involves a combination of experimental and computational approaches to the study of forecasting and decision-making problems in power systems.

	Specifically, based on earlier work, the first goal is to further explore schemes for scenario forecasting through advanced DGMs. To this end, a preliminary problem inquiry and exploration plan are performed to improve the feasibility of deep learning-based forecast in the actual industry.
	My second goal is to systematically design new decision optimization schemes and resolving strategies, making it possible to put scenarios into actual power grid decision-makings, and thereby improve the capability of the overall decision-making work.
